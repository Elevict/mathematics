# 6.1 Inner Product, Length, and Orthogonality

> [!definition] **Definition** (Inner Product)
> The inner product or dot product of 2 vectors $u = \langle u_{1}, \dots, u_{n} \rangle$ and $v = \langle v_{1}, \dots, v_{n} \rangle$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ is the scalar defined by: ${} u, v = u_{1}v_{1} + u_{2}v_{2} + \dots + u_{n}v_{n}{}$

## Properties of the Inner Product

If $u, v, w$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ and $c$ is a scalar

$$
\begin{align}
u \cdot v &= v \cdot u \\
(u+v)\cdot w &= u \cdot w + v \cdot w \\
(cu) \cdot  v &= c(u \cdot  v) = u\cdot (cv) \\
u \cdot u &\geq 0 \text{ and } u \cdot  u = 0 \text{ if and only if } \text{$u$ = 0}
\end{align}
$$

> [!definition] **Definition** (Length of a Vector)
> If $u = \langle u_{1}, u_{2}, \dots, u_{n} \rangle$, the length or norm of $u$ is $\lVert u \rVert = \sqrt{u_{1}^{2} + \dots + u_{n}^{2}}$. If $\lVert u \rVert = 1$, then $u$ is called a unit vector.

> [!definition] **Definition** (Distance Between Two Vectors)
> If $u$ and $v$ are 2 vectors in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$, the distance between $u$ and $v$ is $d(u, v) = ||u - v||$

> [!definition] **Definition** (Orthogonal Vectors)
> $u$ and $v$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ are orthogonal if $u \cdot v = 0$.

> [!theorem] **Theorem** (Pythagorean Theorem)
> $u$ and $v$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ are orthogonal if and only if $\lVert u+v \rVert^{2} = \lVert u \rVert^{2} + \lVert v \rVert^{2}$

> [!definition] **Definition** (Orthogonal Complements)
> Let $W$ be a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ and $u$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$, if $u$ is orthogonal to every vector in $W$, we say $u$ is orthogonal to $W$.
>
> The set of all vectors $u$ that are orthogonal to $W$ is called the orthogonal complement of $W$, and is denoted by $W^{\perp}$.
