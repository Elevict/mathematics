# The Convolution Integral

> [!definition] **Definition** (Convolution Integral)
> If functions $f$ and $g$ are piecewise continuous on the interval $[0, \infty)$, then a special product, denoted by $f \star g$, is defined by the integral
>
> $$
> f \star g = \int_{0}^{t}f(v)g(t-v)dv = \int_{0}^{t}f(t-v)g(v)dv
> $$
>
> and is called the convolution of $f$ and $g$.

Note that $L\{ f \star g \} = L\{ f(t) \} \cdot L\{ g(t) \}$ is a distinct property of the convolution.

> [!problem] **Problem** (Problem 1)
> Evaluate $L \left\{   \int_{0}^{t}e^{v}\sin(t-v)dv  \right\}$
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> L \left\{   \int_{0}^{t}e^{v}\sin(t-v)dv  \right\} &= L\{ e^{v} \} \cdot  L\{ \sin(t-v) \} \\
> &= \frac{1}{s-1} \cdot  \frac{1}{s^{2} + 1}
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!problem] **Problem** (Problem 2)
> Evaluate $L\left\{   \int_{0}^{t} f(v) dv  \right\}$
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> L\left\{   \int_{0}^{t} f(v) dv  \right\} &= L\{ f(t) \star 1 \}  \\
> &= L\{ f(t) \} \cdot  L\{ 1 \}  \\
> &= \frac{F(s)}{s}
> \end{align}
> $$

> [!problem] **Problem** (Problem 3)
> Solve $f(t) = 3t^{2} - e^{-t} - \int_{0}^{t} f(v)e^{t-v}dv$ for $f(t)$.
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> f(t) &= 3t^{2} - e^{-t} - \int_{0}^{t} f(v)e^{t-v}dv \\
> F(s) &= 3L\{ t^{2} \} - L\{ e^{-t} \} - L\{ f(t) \} \cdot  L\{ e^{t} \} \\
> &= \frac{6}{s^{3}} - \frac{1}{s+1} - F(s) \cdot \frac{1}{s-1} \\
> &= \frac{6s^{2} - 6s + 6s - 6 - s^{4} + s^{3}}{s^{4}(s+1)} \\
> &= \frac{-s^{4}+s^{3}+6s^{2}-6}{s^{4}(s+1)}
> \end{align}
> $$
