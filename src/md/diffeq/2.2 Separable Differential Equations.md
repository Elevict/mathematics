# Separable Differential Equations

A first-order ODE of the form $\frac{dy}{dx} = g(x)h(y)$ is said to be separable.

**Problem 1:** Solve $(1+x)dy - ydx = 0$

$$
\begin{align}
(1+x)dy - ydx &= 0 \\
(1+x)dy &= ydx \\
\frac{1}{y} dy &= \frac{1}{1+x} dx \\
\int \frac{1}{y} dy &= \int \frac{1}{1+x} dx \\
\ln \lvert y \rvert + C_1 &= \ln \lvert 1+x \rvert + C_2 \\
\ln \lvert y \rvert &= \ln \lvert 1+x \rvert + C \\
\ln \left\lvert \frac{y}{1+x} \right\rvert &= C \\
\frac{y}{1+x} &= e^C \\
y &= C(1+x)
\end{align}
$$

**Problem 2:** Solve $\frac{dy}{dx} = y^2 - 4$

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{dy}{dx} &= y^2 - 4 \\
\frac{1}{y^2 - 4} dy &= dx \\
\int \frac{1}{y^2 - 4} dy &= \int dx \\
\frac{1}{y^2 - 4} &= \frac{A}{y-2} + \frac{B}{y+2} \\
1 &= A(y+2) + B(y-2) \\
1 &= Ay + 2A + By - 2B \\
1 &= y(A + B) + (2A - 2B) \\
&\begin{cases}
A + B = 0 \\
2A - 2B = 1
\end{cases} \\
A &= -\frac{1}{4}, \quad B = \frac{1}{4} \\
\int \frac{1}{y^2 - 4} dy &= \int \left( \frac{-\frac{1}{4}}{y-2} + \frac{\frac{1}{4}}{y+2} \right) dy \\
&= -\frac{1}{4} \ln |y-2| + \frac{1}{4} \ln |y+2| + C \\
\int dx &= x + C_1 \\
-\frac{1}{4} \ln |y-2| + \frac{1}{4} \ln |y+2| &= x + C_2 \\
\ln \left| \frac{y+2}{y-2} \right| &= 4x + C \\
\frac{y+2}{y-2} &= e^{4x+C} \\
\frac{y+2}{y-2} &= Ce^{4x} \\
y &= \frac{2Ce^{4x} + 2}{Ce^{4x} - 1}
\end{align}
$$

**Problem 3:** Solve the initial-value problem $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x}{y}, \, y(4) = -3$

$$
\begin{align}
\frac{dy}{dx} &= -\frac{x}{y} \\
y \, dy &= -x \, dx \\
\int y \, dy &= -\int x \, dx \\
\frac{1}{2}y^2 + C_1 &= -\frac{1}{2}x^2 + C_2 \\
\frac{1}{2}y^2 &= -\frac{1}{2}x^2 + C \\
y^2 + x^2 &= C \\
(-3)^2 + 4^2 &= C \\
9 + 16 &= C \\
C &= 25 \\
x^2 + y^2 &= 25
\end{align}
$$
