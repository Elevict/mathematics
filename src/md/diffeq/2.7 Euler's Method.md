# Euler's Method

Euler's method is that for a sufficiently small change in $x$, we have

$$
\frac{dy}{dx} \approx \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} \to \Delta y
 = \frac{dy}{dx} \Delta x$$

Consider the initial value problem, $y' = 0.1 \sqrt{y} + 0.4x^{2}, \ y(2) = 4$. Use Euler's method to obtain an approximation of $y(2.5)$ using $\Delta x = 0.1$.

| $x$ | $y$    | $\Delta y = \frac{dy}{dx} \Delta x$ | Computations of $\Delta y$                                                        |
| --- | ------ | ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 2.0 | 4.0000 | 0.1800                              | $\Delta y = [0.1 \sqrt{4} + 0.4(2^2)] \cdot 0.1 = [0.2 + 1.6] \cdot 0.1 = 0.1800$ |
| 2.1 | 4.1800 | 0.1968                              | $\Delta y = [0.1 \sqrt{4.18} + 0.4(2.1^2)] \cdot 0.1 = 0.1968$                    |
| 2.2 | 4.3768 | 0.2145                              | $\Delta y = [0.1 \sqrt{4.3768} + 0.4(2.2^2)] \cdot 0.1 = 0.2145$                  |
| 2.3 | 4.5914 | 0.2330                              | $\Delta y = [0.1 \sqrt{4.5914} + 0.4(2.3^2)] \cdot 0.1 = 0.2330$                  |
| 2.4 | 4.8244 | 0.2524                              | $\Delta y = [0.1 \sqrt{4.8244} + 0.4(2.4^2)] \cdot 0.1 = 0.2524$                  |
