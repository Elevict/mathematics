# Linear Differential Equations

A first order ordinary differential equation of the form $a_{1}(x) \frac{dy}{dx} + a_{0}(x)y = g(x)$ is said to be a linear equation in the variable $y$.

By dividing both sides of the above equation by the lead coefficient, we obtain the standard form of a linear equation.

$$
\frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{a_{0}(x)}{a_{1}(x)}y = \frac{g(x)}{a_{1}(x)} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = F(x)
$$

Solving a linear first-order ordinary differential equation:
1. Remember to put the linear equation in standard form
2. From the standard form of the equation identify $P(x)$ and then find the integrating factor $e^{\int P(x) \ dx}$. No constant need be used in evaluating the indefinite integral $\int P(x) \ dx$.
3. Multiply both sides of the standard form equation by the integrating factor. The left side of the resulting equation is automatically the derivative of the product of the integrating factor and $y$: $\frac{d}{dx}\left[e^{\int P(x) \ dx} y\right] = e^{\int P(x) \ dx}f(x)$
4. Integrate both sides of the above equation and solve for $y$.

**Problem 1:**

$$
\begin{align}
x \frac{dy}{dx} - 4y &= x^{6}e^{x} \\
\frac{dy}{dx} - 4 \frac{y}{x} &= x^{5}e^{x} \\
P(x) &= - \frac{4}{x} \\
\int P(x) \ dx &= -4 \ln \lvert x \rvert  \\
\int P(x) \ dx &= \ln \lvert x^{-4} \rvert  \\
e^{\int P(x) \ dx} &= x^{-4} \\
\frac{d}{dx}[x^{-4}y] &= x^{-4}[x^{5}e^{x}] \\
\frac{d}{dx}[x^{-4}y] &= xe^{x} \\
x^{-4}y &= \int xe^{x} \ dx \\
x^{-4}y &= xe^{x} - \int e^{x} \ dx \\
x^{-4}y &= xe^{x} - e^{x} + C \\
y &= x^{5}e^{x} - x^{4}e^{x} + Cx^{4}
\end{align}
$$

**Problem 2:**

$$
\begin{align}
(x^{2} - 9) \frac{dy}{dx} + xy &= 0 \\
\frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{xy}{x^{2} - 9} &= 0 \\
\int P(x) \ dx &= \int \frac{x}{x^{2} - 9} \ dx \\
\text{Let } u = x^{2} - 9, &  \text{ then} \ du = 2x  \ dx \\
\int \frac{1}{2u} \ du &= \frac{1}{2}\ln \lvert u \rvert  \\
e^{\int P(x)} &= \sqrt{u} = \sqrt{x^{2} - 9} \\
\frac{d}{dx}[(x^{2} - 9)^{1/2}y] &= 0 \\
\sqrt{x^{2} - 9}y &= \int 0 \ dx \\
\sqrt{x^{2} - 9y} &= C \\
y &= \frac{C}{\sqrt{x^{2}} - 9y}
\end{align}
$$

Solve the initial value problem: $\frac{dy}{dx} + y = x, \quad y(0) = 4$

$$
\begin{align}
\int P(x) \ dx &= \int 1 dx \\
e^{\int P(x) \ dx} &= e^{x} \\
\frac{d}{dx}[e^{x}y] &= e^{x}x \\
e^{x}y &= \int e^{x}x \ dx \\
e^{x}y &= xe^{x} - \int e^{x} \ dx \\
e^{x}y &= xe^{x} - e^{x} + C \\
y &= x - 1 + \frac{C}{e^{x}} \\
4 &= 0 - 1 + \frac{C}{e^{0}} \\
C &= 5 \\
y &= x - 1 + 5e^{-x}
\end{align}
$$
