# Impulse Functions

> [!definition] **Definition** (Dirac Delta Function)
> The Dirac delta function is characterized by the following properties:
>
> $$
> \delta(t) = \begin{cases}
> 0 & t \neq 0 \\
> \infty  & t = 0
> \end{cases}
> $$
>
> and
>
> $$
> \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t)\delta(t)dt = f(0)
> $$

Note that

$$
\begin{align}
\delta(t-a) = \begin{cases}
0, & t \neq a \\
\infty, & t = a
\end{cases} \\
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(t) \delta(t-a)dt = f(a)
\end{align}
$$

and,

$$
L \{ \delta(t-a) \} = e^{-as}
$$

> [!problem] **Problem** (Problem 1)
> Solve $y'' + y = 4\delta(t-2\pi)$ subject to $y(0) = 1, y'(0) = 0$
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> s^{2}Y(s) - sy(0) - y(0) + Y(s) &= 4e^{-2\pi s} \\
> Y(s)(s^{2} + 1) &= s + 4e^{-2\pi s} \\
> Y(s) &= \frac{s}{s^{2} + 1} + \frac{4e^{-2\pi s}}{s^{2} + 1} \\
> y(t) &= \cos(t) + 4 \sin(t-2\pi)u(t-2\pi) \\
> y(t) &= \cos(t) + 4\sin(t)u(t-2\pi)
> \end{align}
> $$
