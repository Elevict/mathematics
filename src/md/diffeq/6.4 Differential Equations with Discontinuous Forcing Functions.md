# Differential Equations with Discontinuous Forcing Functions

> [!problem] **Problem** (Problem 1)
> Find the solution of the differential equation
>
> $$
> 2y'' + y' + 2y = g(t)
> $$
>
> where
>
> $$
> g(t) = u_{5}(t) - u_{20}(t) = \begin{cases}
> 1, & 5 \leq t < 20 \\
> 0, & 0 \leq t < 5 \text{ and } t\geq 20
> \end{cases}
> $$
>
> Assume that the initial conditions are
>
> $$
> y(0) = 0, \quad y'(0) = 0
> $$
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> 2y'' + y' + 2y &= g(t) \\
> 2y'' + y' + 2y &= u_{5}(t) - u_{20}(t) \\
> 2s^{2}Y(s) - 2sy(0) - 2y'(0) + sY(s) - y(0) + 2Y(s) &= \frac{e^{-5s}}{s} - \frac{e^{-20s}}{s} \\
> Y(s) &= \frac{e^{-5s}-e^{-20s}}{s(2s^{2} + s + 2)}
> \end{align}
> $$
>
> Now we can conveniently find $y = \phi(t)$ by writing it as,
>
> $$
> Y(s) = (e^{-5s} - e^{-20s})H(s),
> $$
>
> where
>
> $$
> H(s) = \frac{1}{s(2s^{2} + s + 2)}
> $$
>
> Then, if $h(t) = L ^{-1} \{ H(s) \}$, we have
>
> $$
> y = \phi(t) = u_{5}(t)h(t-5) - u_{20}(t)h(t-20)
> $$
>
> and therefore,
>
> $$
> \begin{align}
> H(s) &= \frac{a}{s} + \frac{bs + c}{2s^{2} + s + 2} \\
> &= \frac{1/2}{s} - \frac{s + \frac{1}{2}}{2s^{2} + s + 2} \\
> \end{align}
> $$
>
> which can then be inverse Laplace transformed to get our answer,
>
> $$
> h(t) = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}[e^{-t/4}\cos(\sqrt{15}t/4) + (\sqrt{15}/15)e^{-t/4}\sin(\sqrt{15}t/4)]
> $$
